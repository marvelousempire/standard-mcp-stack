# Security and Authority Model

Security answers who may do what, using which assets, under what conditions, with what evidence.

## Trust lens

Authority comes from the governing instrument and valid delegation. Possession of a key, seal, or account does not by itself create unlimited authority.

## Business lens

Authentication verifies identity. Authorization verifies permissions. Approval verifies that a particular high-risk action has been intentionally accepted.

## Layers

1. **Identity** — who or what is acting?
2. **Authentication** — how is identity proven?
3. **Authorization** — what may that identity do?
4. **Delegation** — what temporary authority has been assigned?
5. **Approval** — has this particular action been accepted?
6. **Custody** — who controls credentials and assets?
7. **Audit** — what evidence is retained?

## Authority levels

- Reception: accept and route
- Director: plan and interpret governance
- Manager: delegate, allocate, and approve within limits
- Worker: execute only the work order
- Tool/server: perform only declared operations

## Least-privilege rule

Every worker, client, server, credential, and tool must receive the narrowest permissions sufficient for the active work order.
